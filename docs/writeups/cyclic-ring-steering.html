The mechanism

Why a line ties on a line — and loses on a loop

This is the same routing test that came back ≈ tied on eleven ordinal concepts. The geometry explains both results at once.

Ordinal concept: the chord is the path

result: linear ties → manifold dominated

A 1-D ordered concept embeds on a (gently curved) line. The straight chord between its endpoints stays on the data manifold, so it induces the same graded slide the spline does. Eleven of eleven census ordinals: manifold − linear ≈ 0.

Cyclic concept: the chord is a diameter

result: manifold 1.00 vs linear 0.29 → non-dominated

The year closes a loop, and January → July is half of it — the straight chord is a diameter, the farthest any path can get from the data. The model reads the nearest endpoint and the walk snaps Jan, Jan, Jan, Jan, Jul, Jul, Jul. Only the arc visits February through June.

Against the controls

Our rings against the controls

Per-waypoint argmax agreement around each ring; the gate requires the manifold to beat the linear chord and the shuffled-order control. Two waves — the census atlas (months, zodiac, color wheel) and an ideated zoo with pre-registered predictions (Chinese zodiac, card ranks) — give the five showcased wins; a sixth, the compass, passes only weakly (0.60, cardinals only) and lives in the strip below rather than here. The fails each catch a named failure mode. Days-of-week — the ring Goodfire's papers already showed — is kept as the protocol baseline, not claimed as a discovery.

What this buys in practice

Ordinal knobs — certainty, formality, quality — are real, but a linear steer delivers them as well or better; ship the cheap thing. The manifold is the right tool where the concept's structure is cyclic: calendars, zodiacs, color wheels, headings, clocks — anywhere "between A and B" means around, not across. Four such rings now demonstrate it, with two honest counterexamples that the controls caught. That, plus read-out (the fitted geometry as a monitor coordinate), is the utility statement for concept manifolds at 2B.

Scope & honesty

What this result does not claim

Reading ≠ routing — in both directions

Nearest-neighbour ring-adjacency (the reading metric) and routing dissociate both ways: the days ring folds onto a single linear direction at layer 12 (1-D adjacency 1.0), while months and zodiac route 1.00 with top-plane adjacency of only 0.50 and 0.42 — the 3-D projection tangles, the walk doesn't. Routing is the decisive evidence; the plots are a faithful projection, not the whole space.

The fails are the controls working

Every small ring (n ≤ 7) that routes 1.00 — time-of-day, solfège, the engine cycle, even the political-horseshoe fold (which walks communist → … → fascist in perfect order) — is caught by a shuffled control that also routes 1.00: with few points in a small region, any smooth path crosses the right neighborhoods. Moon phases and the cell cycle are steer-resistant (rare or multi-word values). And a seam-specific note: on a seam route whose shuffled waypoints land on integer params, the shuffled arm sits on exact centroids and saturates for any well-tokenized concept — there, the informative comparisons are the chord and the closure geometry, not the shuffle.

One model, one seed — but no longer one ring

The mechanism now replicates across six net-new rings beyond the days baseline, all on Qwen3.5-2B (MLX, single seed), waypoints at the item centroids, full-string read-out. The chord's failure is the ring's interior — off-centroid generalization between values is not tested here. Other models and seeds remain the natural follow-up.
