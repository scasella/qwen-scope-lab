The ring a straight line can't follow

Every ordinal concept we tested let a plain linear steer match the manifold — a straight chord interpolates through the middle of a line for free. The week is not a line. On the days-of-week ring, the manifold walks Monday → Friday through every weekday in order; the straight chord cuts across the ring's empty interior and skips the middle of the week entirely.

The mechanism

Why a line ties on a line — and loses on a loop

This is the same routing test that came back ≈ tied on eleven ordinal concepts. The geometry explains both results at once.

Ordinal concept: the chord is the path

result: linear ties → manifold dominated

A 1-D ordered concept embeds on a (gently curved) line. The straight chord between its endpoints stays on the data manifold, so it induces the same graded slide the spline does. Eleven of eleven census ordinals: manifold − linear ≈ 0.

Cyclic concept: the chord leaves the manifold

result: manifold 1.00 vs linear 0.40 → non-dominated

The week closes a loop. A straight chord between Monday and Friday crosses the ring's interior, where no day lives — the model reads the nearest endpoint and the walk snaps Mon, Mon, Fri, Fri, Fri. Only the arc traverses the order.

What this buys in practice

Ordinal knobs — certainty, formality, quality — are real, but a linear steer delivers them as well or better; ship the cheap thing. The manifold is the right tool where the concept's structure is cyclic or near-cyclic: calendars, clocks, compass headings, hue wheels, phase cycles — anywhere “between A and B” means around, not across. That, plus read-out (using the fitted geometry as a monitor coordinate), is the honest utility statement for concept manifolds at 2B.

Scope & honesty

What this result does not claim

Reading ≠ routing

The claim rests on routing (traversing the order), not reading. Nearest-neighbour ring-adjacency is layer-dependent: at layer 12 a single linear direction folds the ring perfectly (1-D adjacency 1.0). A line can represent the week at some layers; it provably cannot walk it.

One ring, one seed

This is days-of-week on one model (Qwen3.5-2B, MLX, single seed), five waypoints, full-string read-out. The mechanism — a chord cannot follow a loop — is geometric and should generalize to time-of-day, months, compass, hues; that replication has not been run yet.

Prompt choice matters

The routing read-out uses a position prompt (“Today is ⟨day⟩. So today is”) so the model reports the injected day rather than its successor. The census ordinals used generic carrier prompts, so their routing numbers are a floor — but the recovery diagnostic's graded metric closed that door: linear matches the manifold there at every granularity.

Shuffled at 0.80

The shuffled-order control scores high on a 7-cycle because many scrambles preserve chunks of adjacency. The gate handles this — the manifold must strictly beat it, and 1.00 > 0.80 — but a longer ring (months, 12 points) would separate the arms further and is the natural follow-up.
